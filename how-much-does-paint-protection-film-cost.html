How Much Does Paint Protection Film Cost? A 2026 Pricing Guide

Paint Protection Film July 19, 2026 10 min read

Quick Answer

Professional paint protection film typically costs about $600–$1,500 for partial-front coverage, $2,000–$3,500 for a full-front package, $2,500–$4,500 for a track package, and $5,000–$10,000+ for full-body PPF. Final pricing depends on vehicle size, panel complexity, paint condition, film choice, edge wrapping, disassembly, and installer quality.

Paint protection film pricing can feel confusing because two vehicles receiving the same general package may still have very different totals. A compact sedan with a smooth front bumper is faster and less material-intensive than a large SUV with complex vents, sensors, trim, and wide body panels.

The price also reflects more than the film itself. Professional PPF installation includes inspection, washing, decontamination, paint preparation, pattern selection, cutting, installation, edge finishing, final inspection, and follow-up quality control.

Average Paint Protection Film Cost

For most vehicles, professional PPF installation falls into four common pricing ranges:

These are realistic professional-installation ranges, not guaranteed quotes. Large trucks, exotic vehicles, satin finishes, custom body kits, and vehicles requiring extensive disassembly can exceed the upper end.

Best Value for Most Owners

A full-front package usually provides the best balance between price and protection because it covers the panels that receive the majority of rock chips and road debris without the cost of wrapping the entire vehicle.

PPF Pricing by Package

Partial-Front PPF: $600–$1,500

A partial-front package commonly includes the front bumper, mirror caps, and a section of the hood and front fenders. It lowers the initial cost, but creates visible film lines across the hood and fenders.

Full-Front PPF: $2,000–$3,500

Full-front protection usually includes the entire hood, complete front fenders, front bumper, mirrors, and sometimes headlights. This is the most popular package for new daily drivers, luxury vehicles, highway commuters, and Teslas.

Track Package: $2,500–$4,500

A track or high-impact package extends protection to rocker panels, lower doors, A-pillars, roof leading edge, and rear wheel impact zones. It is useful for performance cars, wide-tire vehicles, and rough-road driving.

Full-Body PPF: $5,000–$10,000+

Full-body PPF covers nearly every painted exterior panel. Pricing rises because of material use, labor time, complex panel work, edge finishing, and possible disassembly. It is most common on exotic, luxury, collectible, matte-finish, and long-term ownership vehicles.

What Affects the Cost of PPF?

Vehicle Size

Larger vehicles require more film and more installation time. A compact coupe generally costs less than a large SUV or full-size truck.

Panel Complexity

Deep vents, sharp body lines, parking sensors, trim pieces, grilles, spoilers, and complex bumpers increase labor.

Paint Condition

PPF is transparent, so defects remain visible underneath it. Used paint may require decontamination, polishing, touch-up work, or correction before installation.

Film Brand and Finish

Premium gloss, satin, matte, colored, and specialty films differ in material cost, clarity, self-healing performance, stain resistance, and warranty.

Wrapped Edges and Disassembly

Wrapping edges creates a cleaner appearance, but some edges require removal of trim, emblems, lights, handles, or other components.

Installer Experience

Experienced installers charge for consistency, preparation, clean working conditions, pattern control, difficult panel handling, and quality control.

Cheap PPF vs. a Quality Installation

A low quote may be legitimate, but the customer should understand what is included. Some lower-cost packages use partial coverage, exposed edges, simpler patterns, less preparation, or fewer wrapped edges.

Ask What Is Included

Confirm whether the quote includes full hood and fenders, mirrors, headlights, wrapped edges, paint preparation, badge or trim removal, taxes, warranty, and a follow-up quality-control appointment.

Possible Additional PPF Costs

  • Paint correction or polishing before film installation
  • Removal and replacement of old PPF
  • Adhesive removal from previous film
  • Touch-up paint for existing chips
  • Custom templates for aftermarket parts
  • Removal and reinstallation of emblems or trim
  • Ceramic coating applied over the film
  • Extra rocker, door-cup, luggage-area, or rear-impact coverage

Is Paint Protection Film Worth the Cost?

PPF is worth the cost when preserving original paint matters and the vehicle is regularly exposed to highway debris, gravel, winter road material, parking-lot contact, or heavy daily use. It is particularly valuable for new vehicles, dark paint, high-value vehicles, and long-term ownership.

How to Choose the Right PPF Budget

  • Under $1,500: protect the bumper, mirrors, and leading edges.
  • $2,000–$3,500: choose a full-front package for the strongest everyday value.
  • $3,000–$4,500: add rocker panels and other high-impact areas.
  • $5,000+: consider full-body protection for maximum preservation.

Drivers in Allentown, Bethlehem, Easton, and Quakertown should choose coverage based on commute, road conditions, vehicle type, and ownership plans.

Final Answer: How Much Does PPF Cost?

Most professional PPF projects cost between $600 and $3,500 for partial-front or full-front coverage. Track packages often range from $2,500 to $4,500, while full-body installations commonly begin around $5,000 and can exceed $10,000.
